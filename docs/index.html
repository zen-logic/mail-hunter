$ mail-hunter
An email archive that unifies decades of mail.

Old IMAP servers you’re afraid to close. MBOX exports from providers that no longer exist. EML files rescued from old machines. Mail Hunter pulls them all into one searchable, deduplicated, compressed archive on your own hardware — before the next provider shuts down.

> ./get-mailhunter

Why Mail Hunter

One archive, every account

Connect live IMAP servers for incremental sync. Import EML files and MBOX exports for everything else. Twenty years of email across a dozen providers, searchable from a single browser tab.

Your mail, your disk

Every message is stored locally in a content-addressable archive. Deduplicated by SHA-256 hash, compressed with zstd. No cloud dependency, no subscription, no provider lock-in. Runs on any machine with Python.

Search everything at once

Search across all servers, all folders, all years. Filter by sender, recipient, subject, body text, or date range. Results come back fast because the index is local.

Features

IMAP Sync

Incremental sync via UID tracking. Respects UIDVALIDITY changes. Sync progress streamed live over WebSocket. Cancel and resume at any time.

Import

Drag and drop EML files or MBOX archives. Automatic address matching to existing servers. Unmatched addresses prompt for resolution before import.

Deduplication

Messages are hashed on content. Identical messages across servers and folders are stored once. Duplicate counts shown in the UI.

Compression

Raw EML files compressed with zstd at level 19. Typical 70–85% size reduction. Legacy uncompressed files continue to work without migration.

Tagging

Tag messages with arbitrary labels. Organise across server and folder boundaries. Your taxonomy, not your provider’s.

Legal Hold

Place individual messages on legal hold to prevent deletion. For compliance, disputes, or anything you can’t afford to lose.

Message Preview

View HTML, plain text, and raw source in a sandboxed preview. Download the original EML or individual attachments directly.

Three-Panel UI

Servers and folders on the left, message list in the middle, detail pane on the right. Resizable panels. Keyboard and mouse. Runs in any browser.

Who it’s for

Self-hosters
